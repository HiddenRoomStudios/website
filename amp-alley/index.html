Amp Alley IR Collection

The signature impulse response library from Hidden Room Studios — built by engineers who track, mix, and master every day. Analog-processed, record-ready tones from a deep bench of rare and classic speakers.

Purchase the full collection or lite version — or buy any individual pack.

What sets Amp Alley apart is the capture philosophy. Instead of sterile, raw snapshots, each IR passes through world-class analog outboard EQs and premium signal chains. The result is a tone that sits dramatically closer to a finished mix than typical IRs. Dry captures are included if you prefer a blank canvas.

This collection pulls from a deep bench of rare and classic speakers, captured with high-end microphones inside our dedicated speaker chamber. Every tone is shaped through boutique rack gear and dialed in by our engineering team. From the unmistakable chime of a Fender Deluxe and the punch of classic Marshall cabs, to the massive Emperor and the unmistakable Roland JC-120 — Amp Alley delivers a wide, inspiring tonal palette.

You'll also find our custom-engineered, analog-processed EQ sets — "Bacon," "Crystal," "Rawk Dawg," "Tight Metal" — designed to give you instantly mixable tones without guesswork.

DLX

Deluxe Reverb

This IR captures a stock Jensen-equipped, open-back Fender Deluxe Reverb Reissue. With more than 25 years of natural speaker break-in, this amp delivers the classic Deluxe profile: sparkling cleans, rich edge-of-breakup, and a surprising amount of depth under heavy fuzz or distortion.

The "Dlx Dime" variation pushes the head section into its most reactive state, with bass and treble dimed for unique harmonic interplay you won't get from a neutral capture.

All versions were taken through a Quilter power amp set as flat as possible, driven at full stage volume. Captures were made with Antelope converters at 96k using 90-second sine sweeps, ensuring maximum clarity, consistency, and phase accuracy.

EMP

Emperor Speaker Cab

The Emp IR captures a 2x12 Emperor closed-back cabinet loaded with Ted Weber 12A125 alnico speakers. Known for its cello-like midrange and exceptional note bloom — a standout choice for everything from gritty breakup to saturated distortion.

Its natural top-end rolloff smooths out fizz without losing presence, delivering a polished, musical response straight out of the box.

All captures were made through a Quilter power amp at 96k, 90-second sine sweeps, Antelope converters.

JC

Jazz Chorus

This collection captures a well-broken-in, 1980s Roland JC-120 cabinet with its original factory speakers. For certain models, the amp's own power section was used to retain the characteristic tone shaping unique to the JC-120.

Modifiers include "B" for the engaged bright circuit, "Scp" for a mid-scooped EQ voice, and "V" for the classic Roland bucket-brigade vibrato — essential for recreating the signature JC-120 natural chorus texture.

Antelope converters at 96k and 90-second sine sweeps ensure exceptional clarity, consistency, and phase-accurate detail.

This set excels at delivering massive cleans, authoritative crunch, and high-gain tones that retain clarity under pressure.

GUV — Classic Rock AMP

The Mars Guv IRs focus on the Eminence Governor in the X-loaded Marshall 1960B cabinet. The Governor delivers a Vintage-30-style voice with a smoother top end and without the signature midrange spike.

It pairs exceptionally well with tight high-gain tones while maintaining a balanced, confident midrange — a modern, focused alternative to the EV-12L and Greenback captures.
