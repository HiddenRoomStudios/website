Biography

Patrick Hume is a musician, educator, composer, producer, and multi-instrumentalist with more than 23 years of teaching experience. A lifelong performer and songwriter, he has toured the U.S. in a wide range of projects spanning punk, hardcore, electronic music, jazz, and metal — from early bands like Lords, Brain Banger, LifePartner, and W.N.B.A. to later projects including Analog Cannibal, Count Spacey, and his current band Sporelord. He also performs regularly throughout Louisville as a solo jazz guitarist and in various cover bands.

As a composer, Patrick has created commissioned works for the Louisville Arts Network, Teach Repeat, and the Speed Art Museum. He is the co-owner of Hidden Room Studios, a full-service recording studio in Louisville, Kentucky.

Patrick holds a BA in Music Technology from Bellarmine University and completed post-baccalaureate studies in Music Composition at the University of Louisville. Across teaching, performance, recording, and composition, his work is grounded in a deep appreciation for all genres of music and a commitment to helping others develop their craft.
